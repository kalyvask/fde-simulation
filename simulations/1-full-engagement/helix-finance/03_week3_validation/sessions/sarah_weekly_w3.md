# Sarah Weekly Check-in — Week 3 Monday, 30 min, in-person

> ⚠️ **SYNTHESIZED HYPOTHESIS-CONFIRMING PROXY.** Sarah is the economic buyer (CIO + Managing Partner). Weekly 30-min check-ins keep her informed without burning her time. This is the third weekly. Tone is short, sharp, and outcome-focused.

## Setup

Monday week 3, 8:30 AM ET. Sarah's office, before the trading day. She has a coffee. No printout. She wants a status, not a presentation.

## Selected exchanges

**Sarah**: "Status."

**Alex**: "Three of three sign-off gates are on track for Friday. Eval at 96% pass on 50 cases, variance 3.2% — under the 5% threshold. Mei's biweekly is tomorrow morning; she's done a mock-audit and has two findings I'm shipping by Thursday. Rachel reviews 20 drafts Wednesday; I'm bringing the system through tone-shift architecture revision based on what's coming out of week 2 cases."

**Sarah**: "Tone-shift architecture revision in week 3 is concerning. What's the issue?"

**Alex**: "The Tone Supervisor as built reads the current call's transcript and scores it for sentiment. It doesn't compare to the prior-quarter note. So it's detecting tone, not tone *shift*. Rachel will catch this Wednesday. I'm wiring the prior-quarter note into the supervisor's context this week so we can show her the corrected version in the Wednesday review."

**Sarah**: "OK. You're going to tell me this means we ship Friday, not Monday."

**Alex**: "Worst case yes. Best case still Friday. The revision is a 2-day change. The risk is the 20-draft pass-rate ratio with Rachel — if 18 of 20 don't clear her bar, we iterate before rollout. If they clear, we ship Monday."

**Sarah**: "And if Rachel says 16 of 20?"

**Alex**: "Then we re-scope. Not auto-publish in any phase; treat v1 as analyst-mandatory-approval for the first 6 weeks instead of opt-in. The downside is slower realized productivity gain. The upside is the engagement doesn't have a quality controversy in week 5 that nukes the relationship."

**Sarah**: "Carmen has been asking about this. She thinks the analysts will fight the rollout."

**Alex**: "Carmen's right to surface that. I want to set up a hostile review with her — she role-plays a skeptical trader reading the AI-drafted notes, finds what would make her reject them. I think we can do that Thursday afternoon."

**Sarah**: "Good. Set it up. I want her to be invested, not just CC'd. If she pushes hard, I trust the system more. If she doesn't push at all, that's the bigger problem."

**Alex** *(notes)*: "Booking Carmen for Thursday 3 PM."

**Sarah**: "Last thing. The Q4 board meeting is in week 6. If we ship Friday and have two weeks of production data, I can show the board real numbers — pass rate, adoption rate, time saved per analyst. If we ship later, I'm showing them a Gantt chart. Help me ship Friday."

**Alex**: "Hearing you. Friday is the target. I'll send a written status Wednesday after Rachel's review. If anything moves the date, I'll tell you within 4 hours, not at next Monday's check-in."

**Sarah**: "Good. Anything else?"

**Alex**: "One thing. We're tracking analyst adoption as a separate metric from quality. Target is 70% by week 6. Below that, we treat as adoption problem and re-engage with David on the rollout mechanic, not just the eval bar."

**Sarah**: "Smart. The board cares about realized usage, not pass-rate. Adoption metric is the right one. Send me the dashboard before week 6."

**Alex**: "Done."

*(Wraps at 27 min.)*
